Deadlock

Learn how deadlocks occur in Operating Systems and how they can be prevented.

What is Deadlock?

A Deadlock is a situation in which two or more processes are waitingfor resources held by each other, and none of them can proceed.

Necessary Conditions of Deadlock

  • Mutual Exclusion
  • Hold and Wait
  • No Preemption
  • Circular Wait

Example of Deadlock

Process P1 holds Resource A and waits for Resource B. Process P2 holds Resource B and waits for Resource A. Both processes wait forever.

Methods to Handle Deadlock

  • Deadlock Prevention
  • Deadlock Avoidance
  • Deadlock Detection
  • Deadlock Recovery

Prevention Techniques

  • Allocate all resources at once
  • Allow resource preemption
  • Avoid circular waiting
  • Use proper scheduling
